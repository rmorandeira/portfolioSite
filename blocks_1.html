Inspired by the dazzle camouflage of the First World War ships, the wall plays with the perspective and the vanishing point, breaking the siloutte of the building as creates a thirdimensional effect. (SeeAn Illustrated History of Unbelievably Camouflaged Ships)



Also, the architechtonical influencies of the De Stijl are present in the sketches, as the final wall paint.



Nowadays, the wall artist Mary Iverson also plays with perspective, the landscape and influence of the shipping industry of his hometown, Seattle.
